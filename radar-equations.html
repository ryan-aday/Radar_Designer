Radar equations, resolution, and RCS

Core formulas for link closure, resolution cells, weather radar scaling, and radar cross section estimates.

Link equations

One-way link (warners/secondary)

PRX = PTX·GTX·GRX·λ² / (4πR)²

Use for radar warning receivers, secondary radars, or comms; omit σ.

Two-way radar (monostatic)

PRX = (PTX·G²·λ²·σ)/((4π)³·R⁴·Ltot)

Losses (page 45): transmit/receive lines, beamshape, integration, fluctuation (Swerling), processing.

Weather radar variant

Received power scales with 1/R² because pulse volume grows with range: PRX ∝ Z·PTX·G²·λ² / ((4π)²·R²), where Z is reflectivity.

Resolution cell

Range resolution

ΔR = c0·τ/2 for simple pulses; for compressed pulses, replace τ with compressed width or bandwidth relation ΔR = c0/(2·BW).

Angular/volume

Resolution cell volume: V = R²·θAz·θEl·c0τ/2. Angular separation improves with narrower beamwidths; monopulse can resolve targets inside the 3 dB beam.

Pulse volume & weather

As V grows with R², the number of scatterers increases, shifting the range dependence from R⁴ to R² in weather radar equation.

RCS regions & reflections

Rayleigh / Mie / optical

Rayleigh: target size ≪ λ, σ ∝ λ-4. Mie: resonance oscillations (±6 dB swings). Optical: geometric optics dominates, σ ≈ projected area.

Creeping & cavity returns

Complex targets combine tip diffraction, creeping waves, cavity resonances, and edge diffraction (Fig 39). σ can fluctuate >30 dB; model with Swerling cases in link budgets.

Retroreflectors

Corner reflectors and van Atta arrays redirect energy back to source; increasing one large reflector is more efficient than multiple small ones.

Decibels & EIRP

dB refresher

L = 10 log10(Pout/Pin); for voltages/currents use 20·log terms. Common references: dBm (1 mW), dBW (1 W), dBi (isotropic).

EIRP

EIRP = PTX + GTX (dBm/dBi). Use when comparing against spectral masks or jamming power.

Noise temperature

Te = 290·(10^{NF/10} - 1). Receiver sensitivity: S = kTB + NF + margin.
