Propagation

Link budgets, clearance, and losses

Calculate the path between transmitter and receiver: free-space loss, Fresnel clearance, diffraction, and atmospheric effects.

Link budget (Fig 7)

Power accounting

PRX = EIRP - LTX - FSPL - Latm - Lmisc + GRX - LRX (dB)

Losses include cables, polarization mismatch, fading margin, and receiver line loss. Saturation/MDS set usable dynamic range.

FSPL

FSPL = 20 log10(4πR/λ). Use λ = c/f. Below Fresnel distance, FSPL dominates; beyond it, consider two-ray interference.

Polarization loss

Mismatched polarization adds Lpol (typ. a few dB). Align linear/circular senses to preserve gain.

Fresnel & earth bulge (Figs 8–9)

First Fresnel zone radius

b = √(λ·d1·d2/(d1+d2)). Keep ≥60% of the zone clear; 40% blockage can fully fade the link.

Fresnel distance

FZ = 0.5·√(hTX·hRX·λ). If link distance < FZ, FSPL applies; otherwise move to two-ray.

Earth bulge

Bulge height: H = d²/66 (d in km, H in m). Use equivalent earth radius 8500 km when computing radar horizon or clearance.

Attenuation (Figs 10–11)

Atmosphere & rain

Specific attenuation rises sharply above 10 GHz. Example: at 10 GHz in heavy rain, 0.12 dB/km (rain) + 0.02 dB/km (molecular) ⇒ reduce 100 km max range to <45 km.

Frequency selection

Consult Fig 10 to choose bands with minimal gaseous and rain loss for long-range search. High-millimeter-wave bands suit short-range, high-resolution tasks.

ISM/radar bands

Fig 1 provides ISM allocations (e.g., 2.4 GHz, 5.8 GHz, 24 GHz) and radar allocations (e.g., 2.7–3.1 GHz, 9.2–9.5 GHz). Match mission with allowed spectrum.

Two-ray model & diffraction (Figs 12–13)

Two-ray path

LOS distance: dLOS = √(R² + (hTX-hRX)²); reflected path: dref = √(R² + (hTX+hRX)²). Phase difference drives constructive/destructive interference.

Two-ray loss

Received power ∝ (hTX·hRX)² / R⁴ when paths cancel. Use when antennas are near ground or R > FZ.

Knife-edge diffraction

Diffraction parameter: ν = H·√(2/λ · (1/d1 + 1/d2)). Loss approximation: LKED = 1.27ν² + 9ν + 6 dB for 0<ν<2.4; 0 dB if ν<0; 13+20 log ν if ν>2.4.
