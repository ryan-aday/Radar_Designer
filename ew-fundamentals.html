EW foundations

Electronic warfare landscape

Condensed definitions and tactics: support, attack, and protection measures that shape radar survivability.

EW subareas

Electronic support (ES)

Detect, intercept, identify, and geolocate EM emitters. Includes SIGINT flavors: ELINT, COMINT, FISINT.

Electronic attack (EA)

Actions to interfere with radar/communications: passive expendables (chaff, decoys) and active jamming (barrier, deception, swept/barrage).

Electronic protection (EP)

Design features that reduce adversary EW effectiveness: sidelobe blanking/cancellation, pulse compression, frequency agility, burn-through modes, monopulse, LPI measures, and EM masking.

Jamming taxonomy

Noise & deception

  • Noise, spot, swept, and barrage jamming (frequency agility stress).
  • Repeater, RGPO/VGPO for range/velocity pull-off.
  • Blip enhancement, cover-pulse, jammer-illuminated chaff (JAFF/CHILL).

Deployment geometries

  • Self-protection: mainlobe jamming with co-located radar/target.
  • Escort: jammer near the target, inside radar beam.
  • Support / sidelobe: jammer outside mainlobe; uses sidelobe gain GS.
  • Stand-in/off: closer/farther than protected aircraft.

Key ratios

Jamming-to-signal ratio: J/S = (PJ·GJ·GR) / (PS·GT·GR) at the victim receiver. Burn-through occurs when S > J at range RBT.

Support jamming math

J/S in sidelobes (Fig 20)

J/S = ERPJ/ERPT + 11 + GM - GS + 40 log RT - 20 log RJ - 10 log f (dB)

Use mainlobe gain GM, average sidelobe gain GS, target range RT, jammer range RJ, and carrier frequency f (MHz).

Burn-through with support jamming

RBT = √( (RT2·RJ·ERPT·GM) / (4·4π·ERPJ·GS) )

Escort jamming collapses to self-protection math if the escort sits inside the radar beam.

Chaff & decoys

Single dipole RCS: σmax ≈ 0.86·λ²; average σ̄ ≈ 0.155·λ². Cloud effectiveness begins ~0.5 s after dispense, lasting up to 30 min. Active decoy RCS: σ ≈ 39 + Gamp - 20 log f (MHz).
