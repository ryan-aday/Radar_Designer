Radar modes, timing, and detection

Compare pulse, FMCW, and advanced modulation options. Use timing equations to size range/velocity windows and dwell requirements.

Pulse radar (Fig 21)

Timing

Pulse repetition time (PRT) includes transmit pulse width τ, dead time, recovery, and receive time. Range from delay: R = c0·td/2.

Blind & unambiguous range (Fig 22)

Blind range: Rblind = c0(τ + tdead + trec)/2. Unambiguous range: Ru = c0(PRT - τ)/2. Staggered PRF shifts ambiguous echoes.

Duty cycle

Duty = τ / PRT. Pulse compression (FMOP/PMOP) keeps high energy while shrinking effective blind zones.

Continuous-wave radar (FMCW / FMiCW)

Unmodulated CW

Measures Doppler only; distance ambiguity beyond λ. Suitable for speed sensors or fixed-distance monitoring.

FMCW distance

With sawtooth sweep: R = c0|Δf|/(2·k), where k = d f(t)/dt is sweep slope. Doppler shift overlays on Δf for moving targets.

FMiCW

Pulsed transmit with continuous oscillator enables higher power and sensitivity while preserving FMCW-style processing.

Modulations & coding

Chirp (FMOP)

Linear up/down chirps increase bandwidth: range resolution ΔR = c0/(2·BW). Long chirps with compression ratios up to hundreds reduce peak power needs.

Phase modulation (PMOP)

Bi-phase (BPSK) and poly-phase (QPSK) codes; Barker codes provide low sidelobes (lengths 2–13). Nesting (e.g., B5×B13) yields length 65.

Peak sidelobes

Peak sidelobe level: PSL = 10 log( max xi² / x0² ). Integrated sidelobe level sums all sidelobe power relative to mainlobe.

Detection metrics

Probability & false alarms

PD = detected / total targets; FAR = false alarms per PRT / range cells. CFAR adapts threshold based on noise/interference.

Dwell & hits per scan

Dwell time: TD = 6·PRT/θAz for a rotating antenna (θ in degrees). Hits per scan: N = TD/(PRT·n) with rotation rate n rps.

Doppler & JEM

Doppler frequency: fD = 2vr/λ. Jet engine modulation adds blade-rate signatures useful for classification.

Height and horizon

Height estimation

H ≈ R·sin e + R²/(2·requiv), where elevation angle e is read at the pedestal and requiv ≈ 8500 km.

Radar horizon

Range to horizon: RNM = 1.23·√hft (nautical miles) or Rkm = 4.12·√hm. For targets above ground: sum antenna and target height terms.

Ducting

Strong negative refractivity gradients trap waves (surface, elevated, evaporation ducts). Leads to extended detection but requires clutter suppression at long ranges.
