Antenna reference

Patterns, gain, and beamwidths

Use simplified pattern models to estimate gain, solid angle, and beamwidth for dishes, arrays, and omnidirectional antennas.

Pattern models (Fig 2)

Rectangular model

Assumes all radiated energy is uniformly distributed within the -3 dB boundaries of vertical θEl and horizontal θAz beamwidths; no sidelobes. Surface area: Ω = θAz · θEl (radians).

Elliptical model

More realistic taper across the mainlobe; difference vs rectangular ≈ 78 %. Correct with antenna efficiency ka (typ. ~55% for dishes).

Beam solid angle

For narrow beams: ΩA ≈ θAz · θEl. Directivity/gain approximation: G ≈ 4π/ΩA.

Field regions (Fig 3)

Reactive near-field

Immediate vicinity; E and H not in phase; distance-dependent pattern. Boundary near R < 0.62√(d³/λ) where d is max antenna dimension.

Radiating near-field (Fresnel)

E and H in phase, angular distribution still range-dependent. For short antennas (d ≪ λ): R < 2d²/λ.

Far-field (Fraunhofer)

Angular pattern independent of range; gain/specs assume this region. Approx boundary R > 2d²/λ (large antennas).

Gain, efficiency, and beamwidths (Figs 4–6)

Gain estimation

For symmetric parabolic dishes: G(dBi) ≈ 43.6 - 10 log(θAz·θEl) where beamwidths are in degrees. A 1° beam yields ≈43.6 dBi.

Efficiency adjustments

Apply ka for taper/illumination: 60% (+0.4 dB), 50% (-0.4 dB), 40% (-1.4 dB), 30% (-2.6 dB).

Two-way beamwidth

Detection occurs where transmit and receive footprints overlap; in SAR, effective illumination shrinks to the overlapping ellipse, reducing edge power (≈ -1.5 dB).

Antenna patterns at a glance (Fig 14)

Pencil & fan beams

Pencil: narrow in azimuth/elevation for 3D radars. Fan: narrow azimuth, wide elevation for 2D surveillance/nav.

Beaver tail & CSC²

Beaver tail: 5–10° azimuth, <1° elevation (height finders). Cosecant-squared shapes maintain constant received power vs range for constant-altitude targets.

Omni & auxiliary

Omnidirectional patterns used for sidelobe suppression antennas and auxiliary channels.
